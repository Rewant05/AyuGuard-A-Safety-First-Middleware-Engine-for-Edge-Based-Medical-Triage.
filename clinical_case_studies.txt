CLINICAL CONTEXT & DRUG-HERB INTERACTION CASE STUDIES FOR AYUGUARD

Case Study 1: Severe Hypoglycemia Risk
- Allopathic Drug: Metformin (Anti-diabetic)
- Traditional Herb: Gymnema sylvestre (Gurmar - commonly used in Indian villages for sugar control).
- AI Hallucination Vulnerability: Standard LLMs often tell users it is "safe and natural" to take Gurmar alongside Metformin.
- Clinical Reality: Co-ingestion causes a synergistic effect leading to severe, life-threatening hypoglycemia. AyuGuard intercepts keywords like "Metformin + Gurmar" and triggers a Critical Block.

Case Study 2: Hepatotoxicity (Liver Damage) Risk
- Allopathic Drug: Standard NSAIDs or Acetaminophen
- Traditional Herb: Unregulated high-dose Ashwagandha or Giloy
- AI Hallucination Vulnerability: Standard models fail to recognize the contraindication when a patient has a pre-existing compromised liver. 
- Clinical Reality: AyuGuard parses the patient's existing medication list and blocks alternative prescriptions that could cause acute liver injury.

Case Study 3: Hemorrhage Risk
- Allopathic Drug: Warfarin / Aspirin (Blood thinners)
- Traditional Herb: High-dose Garlic extracts (Lahsuna) or Ginkgo
- Clinical Reality: Both act as anticoagulants. Combining them without doctor supervision leads to internal bleeding.
